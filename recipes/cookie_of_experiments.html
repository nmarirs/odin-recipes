Cookie of Experiments

About the dish

Mmmm.. cookies for breakfast, cookies for lunch and cookies for dinner

Marvel at the amalgamation of every single chemical reaction from a 11th grade chemistry textbook in just one cookie. Is it safe? Well you'll never know if you dont try.

Ingredients

  • Any 11th grade chemistry textbook, they all are equally bad
  • A well stocked laboratory
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 1 cup peanut butter (fat L if you are allergic)
  • 1 egg
Tip: Bring along a 11th grader to do the work for you by offering food.

Steps

  1. Pre heat an oven to 630K.
  2. Combine sugar, peanut butter, and egg.
  3. Begin your experiments, if the prodcut if a precipitate or a solution, pour it into the mix. Leave the other products aside.
  4. Pass any gaseous products through the mix using tubes.
  5. Shape the dough into balls and place them onto a cookie sheet.
  6. Bake for 644.300 sec and remove the cookies from the oven.
  7. Top the cookie with the solid products you left aside from step 3.
  8. Your cookie might vary depending on the textbook used, but if you got the steps right, it should look radioactive (It isn't radioactive, but trust me when i say that we tried adding Uranium-235 to the recipe. The taste was supbar).
